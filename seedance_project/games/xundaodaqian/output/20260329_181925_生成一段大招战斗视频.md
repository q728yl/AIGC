I anchor the video to @0045.jpg as the locked first frame because this is a mid-battle Xundao Daqian combat UI and the composition, side-on battlefield view, top HUD placement, left-to-right attack flow, and unit arrangement must remain unchanged. I selected only 7 images total to stay well under the 9-image limit: the first-frame lock, one hero ultimate sheet, one spirit-beast assist FX sheet, one enemy recoil sheet, one gold-ink ultimate VFX sheet, and two UI packs for floating damage and HP/status feedback. The timeline starts by resolving the exact moment already captured in the frame: the existing floating damage text fades, the right-side purple avian enemy recoils, and status icons persist. Then the next turn in round 4 triggers a single core action: a left-side ally unleashes a snappy but readable ultimate attack. I apply the Seedance principle of 'slow is smooth' by breaking the ultimate into clear phases—charge, release, impact, recoil, settle—rather than chaotic nonstop motion, while still preserving the game's cutout-animation and turn-based snap. Physics feedback is explicit: the slash travels left-to-right, the boss head and body jerk back, nearby flying units wobble, HP bars drop, crit numbers burst, and golden ink shockwaves dissipate. Because the user wants motion design inspired by online gameplay demos, I blend the practical rhythm of Xundao Daqian real gameplay trailers: short anticipation, exaggerated 2D ink slash, brief screen shake feel, large crit UI feedback, then quick return to idle without over-animating like 3D action games.
