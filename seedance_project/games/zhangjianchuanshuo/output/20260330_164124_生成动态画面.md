## 1. Director's Thought
The first frame is a vertical promotional poster, not live gameplay, so the animation must preserve the original composition, logos, title text, and five-character arrangement instead of inventing enemies or forcing a battle HUD. I selected only essential assets under the 9-image limit: the first frame lock, background parallax split for gentle marketplace depth, secondary motion layers for subtle character cloth/hair movement, blue slash follow-through VFX to continue the visible sword energy naturally, wind leaf particles for environmental motion, logo/title glow FX to enhance readability, and floating sparkles for the upper-right elf-like girl. I applied the 'slow is smooth' principle by using subtle poster-live animation, slight camera punch-in, cloth sway, energy drift, and text glow rather than aggressive action. Physics continuity is handled softly: the already-visible blue slash streak continues to travel left-to-right, leaves drift with wind, cape and sleeves react with delayed secondary motion, and particles dissipate naturally. No new characters, enemies, damage numbers, HP bars, or scene changes are introduced.
